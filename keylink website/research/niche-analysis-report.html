Key Findings

What Separates the Top 10% from the Rest

01

Quantified Proof Dominates Elite Hero Sections

Every site scoring 45+ displays hard numbers above the fold. Not claims — numbers. First Frontier leads with "200,000+ Successful Shipments | 25+ Years Experience" in the first viewport. ET Transport shows 8 certification badges immediately. Sites below 35 use words like "reliable," "trusted," and "quality" — without proof.

Keylink gap: "1,500+ truckloads/month" buried in a bullet list — not a hero stat
02

Compliance Badges = Instant B2B Trust

In freight, buyers are risk-averse. USDOT numbers, MC numbers, insurance amounts, and certifications are decision-making information — not boilerplate. Top performers display these as styled visual badges at the top of the page. ET Transport shows 8 compliance logos in the first scroll. Bottom performers bury them in text blocks or don't show them at all.

Keylink gap: USDOT #2832041, MC# 946449, and $750K BIPD mentioned in text — not shown as badges
03

Multiple Explicit CTAs — Quote + Phone

Top sites use dual conversion paths: "Get a Quote" (form) + a phone number click-to-call. This captures both research-mode buyers (form) and ready-to-book buyers (phone). Direct Way Transport has two phone numbers in their header. First Frontier has "Get a Quote" appearing three times on their homepage.

Keylink gap: No phone number visible in navigation. Single CTA only.
04

Real Photography + Video

Stock photos are the enemy of trust in freight. The best sites use real fleet photography and company culture video. First Frontier uses real truck-on-mountain-highway shots. ET Transport has an embedded YouTube company film that communicates fleet scale and driver quality in 90 seconds. Authentic visuals convert freight brokers — they know what a real fleet looks like.

Keylink gap: Hero video fails to load. AI-generated hero image reduces credibility.
05

Dedicated Service Pages Drive SEO

The top 3 competitors all have individual pages for "FTL Dry Van," "Cross-Border Transport," "LTL," etc. These pages rank for specific high-intent search terms like "FTL carrier BC" and "cross-border dry van Canada-USA." Sites with all services compressed onto one page rank for almost nothing beyond their brand name.

Keylink gap: Zero dedicated service pages. All services listed on homepage only.

Recommended Approach

6 Priorities for the New Site

Ranked by impact. Implementing all 6 would move Keylink from a score of 25 to 50+ — leapfrogging all but one competitor.

1

Make the Numbers Unmissable

Display 1,500+, 90%, $750K, and 5★ as animated stat counters in the hero. This alone will triple the trust signal above the fold. Every top performer does this — Keylink has better numbers than most and doesn't show them.

2

Build Compliance Badge Components

Create styled visual badges for USDOT #2832041, MC# 946449, $750K BIPD Insurance (ICBC), and "Licensed Canada–USA Carrier." Display prominently in the hero section and footer. Freight brokers look for these instantly.

3

Add Phone Number to Navigation

Add (778) 549-8334 to the top-right of every page. This single change typically increases lead conversion by 15–20% for B2B service businesses. Direct Way Transport — a local competitor — has two phone numbers in their header.

4

Create 3 Dedicated Service Pages

Build individual pages for: FTL Dry Van, Cross-Border Transport (Canada–US), and Real-Time Dispatch Support. Each page targets specific high-intent keywords and includes a quote form. This is the primary driver of organic search rankings for the niche.

5

Replace the Hero Visual

Commission a Nano Banana 3D asset (cinematic dark-background truck render) or source real fleet photography. The hero visual is the first trust signal a visitor sees — it must be authentic and premium. An AI-generated image and a broken video do the opposite.

6

Add a Freight Corridors Map

An animated SVG map showing active routes (Vancouver → Calgary, Vancouver → Sacramento, BC → Midwest) communicates scale and capability better than any copy. Shippers and brokers need to see that you cover their lane before they'll reach out.
